Concept

One of my initial roles was to create concept art for our game. Our original idea was to create different worlds with different shapes that the player could morph into. Ultimately because of time constraints we had to cut down on the number of environments we had originally planned for.

Modelling

To split up the work, our team decided to divide up the film according to its 4 major environments. I was responsible for modelling the astronaut, space station, datapad, planets, and the last environment seen in the film.

Animating

As each team member was already assigned one of the four environments, we split up animation in the same way. Everyone was responsible for animating the action taking place in their particular environment. In addition to animating my scenes, I also animated the planet intro at the beginning of the film, and a transition scene between two environments near the beginning.

Editing

I was responsible for adding visual effects to our film. To create backgrounds, I first created a gradient for the sky, and then added stars using After Effects simulations, and finally rays of light for variety.

There were also a few instances where I had to overlay an image we created onto our 3D renders. For example, in one scene, the astronaut opens up a holographic map. I had to overlay the map on top of the rectangle, and made sure it fit the changing shape.

Final

Reflection

This project was full of learning experiences, as it was the first time any of us had used Autodesk Maya. However, as we became more familiar with the program, we were able to split up tasks in a way that let each of us demonstrate our strengths. For example, one member was very good at modelling environments, while another member was able to animate very quickly.

In conclusion, we were able to create an film that although altered in some ways, was faithful to our original concept and vision.
